One of the advantages of the robotic approach is the ability to print on  complex  non-planar  surfaces  typical  of  anatomical  defects.  As  in conventional  Additive  Manufacturing  planning  process,  the  printing path is usually determined by applying the planar slicing to the digital model, consisting of the division of the object into 2D staked layers [21].
